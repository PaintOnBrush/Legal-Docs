A Person is a Fiction in Legalese


From Bouvier's Law Dictionary 1856 ...
“Although man and person are synonymous in grammar, they have a different acceptation in law.”.

From American Law and Procedure, Vol. 13, page 137, 1910 ...
“This word 'person' and its scope and bearing in the law, involving, as it does, legal fictions and also apparently [IN APPEARANCE ONLY] natural beings, , it is difficult to understand; but it is absolutely necessary to grasp, at whatever cost, a true and proper understanding to the word in all the phases of its proper use. A person is here not a physical or individual person, but the status or condition with which he is invested. Not an individual or physical person, but the status, condition or character borne by physical persons. The law of persons is the law of status or condition”.

From Black's Law Dictionary 1st Edition ...

--"A man considered according to the rank he holds in society, with all the rights to which the place he holds entitles him and the duties it imposes". Your person is how you are considered in society. Your "true" essence is not the perception of others "in society". Hence, the "person" is only a conceptualization of others, a fiction, not reality.

--- “A human considered as being capable of having rights and of being charged with duties". A Human considered (thought of) as "only" being capable of having rights... again, this "thought" or perception is a fiction. Start with any human, think of him as being "capable BUT without rights" but definitely charged with duties - the result is a person. It would appear there are some persons who (are considered to) have no rights.

---“A person is such, not because he is human but because rights and duties have been ascribed to him”. The only way rights and duties can be ascribed is by an existing master or by contract. God can ascribe rights and duties to his creations as a master. But God did not ascribe his rights to fictional persons, he ascribed them to man. Any other subsequent rights and duties can only be ascribed by consent and contract. Contract creates rights and duties attributable to the person only. It follows that personal would be likened to contractual. The person is (therefore) man's entity for contract

Interpretation Act - Canada
“person”, or any word or expression descriptive of a person, includes a corporation"

From Bouvier's Law Dictionary 1856 ...
“Although man and person are synonymous in grammar, they have a different acceptation in law.”.

From Black's Law Dictionary, Second Edition (1910), page 577
Man (homo) is a term of nature; person (persona) of civil law. Calvin (.)

God created man... not persons. Persons are a fiction created through a contract or to enable contracting. Your fictional "person" began with the first legal document connected with you - the statement of live birth, which was registered with your government.

A person is a fictional contract entity (corporation) with rank. The rank indicates what rights the person will have (if any) and their duties.
