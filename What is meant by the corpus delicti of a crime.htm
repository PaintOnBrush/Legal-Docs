What is meant by the corpus delicti of a crime?

  • triswmn20k asked 7 years ago
How does the corpus delicti of a crim differ from the statuory elements that must be proved to convict a particular defendent of commiting that crime?
Answer
Follow (0)
Watchlist

Best AnswerVoter's Choice

  • Katie answered 7 years ago
The corpus deliciti is the FACTS that show that a crime occured. In corpus deliciti the courts said that there needs to be 2 things that are proven. 1) a certain result has been produced. and 2) that person is criminally responsible for that action tha has occured. Elements of a crime are the features of the crime. They are basically a list of things that the prosecution has to prove beyond a reasonable doubt that ALL of the items on that list was caused by the defendant. And in most cases, there are more than just 2 things that need to be proven, in order to find the defendant guilty.

Source:

"Criminal Justice" written by: Schmalleger
